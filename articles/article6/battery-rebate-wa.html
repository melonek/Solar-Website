WA Residential Battery Scheme: Rebates, VPP & Loans Explained (2025)

An in-depth guide to the WA Residential Battery Scheme for Perth homeowners, covering state rebates, federal top‑up, VPP participation, no‑interest loans, and how to apply.

Overview of the Scheme

The WA Residential Battery Scheme launched on 1 July 2025 to accelerate the uptake of home energy storage across Western Australia. By reducing upfront costs and providing financial incentives, the program aims to:

  • Lower household energy bills: Shift consumption from expensive peak periods to stored low‑cost solar energy.
  • Strengthen grid reliability: Aggregate small home batteries into a virtual network that can respond instantly to grid disturbances.
  • Maximise renewable usage: Store excess solar generation during the day and dispatch it in the evening.
  • Encourage industry growth: Drive demand for local installers, manufacturers, and technology innovation.

With funding for up to 100,000 systems, the scheme is expected to add around 1 GWh of distributed storage, smoothing peaks and reducing reliance on gas‑fired backup plants.

State Rebate Details

The state rebate is tiered by utility provider:

  • Synergy (metro WA): $130 per kWh, up to 10 kWh → max $1,300.
  • Horizon Power (regional WA): $380 per kWh, up to 10 kWh → max $3,800.

How it works: Your accredited installer submits the rebate claim after installation. You receive the discount directly on your final invoice, so there’s no rebate paperwork for you to handle.

Technical requirements: Systems must have ≥5 kWh usable capacity, be VPP‑capable, equipped with a compatible inverter (listed by Synergy/Horizon), and maintain an internet connection for monitoring and dispatch signals.

Federal Top-Up Support

The Commonwealth’s Cheaper Home Batteries Program tops up state rebates so that:

  • Synergy-area homes receive up to $5,000 total support on a 10 kWh system.
  • Horizon Power homes receive up to $7,500 total support.

Accredited clean energy retailers claim the federal portion at installation, further reducing your out-of-pocket expenses. This combined approach can cut typical 10 kWh system costs by over 80%.

Virtual Power Plant Requirement

Joining a Virtual Power Plant (VPP) is mandatory for rebate recipients. A VPP:

  1. Aggregates home batteries into a single controllable power source.
  2. Provides grid services such as frequency control and peak shaving, often within seconds of a disturbance.
  3. Generates revenue for participants through export payments or bill credits.

Customer benefits: You may earn ~6 ¢/kWh each time your battery discharges during peak events, and you can track performance via an app. This on‑top income can add up to several hundred dollars annually.

Grid benefits: By smoothing demand spikes, VPPs defer the need for expensive peaker plants and reduce blackout risk. Even small contributions (e.g., 100 homes exporting 1 kWh each) can make a real difference.

No-Interest Loan Option

An optional, 0% interest loan of $2,001–$10,000 is available for households earning under $210,000 gross annually. Key points:

  • No fees or collateral – entirely unsecured, fixed 0% interest.
  • Flexible terms – choose a 3–10 year repayment schedule to suit your budget.
  • Use case – covers battery, inverter upgrades, and even additional solar panels when installed together.

Example: A $6,000 loan over 7 years is ~$71/month at 0%, compared to ~$90/month at standard 5% interest. All repayments go to Plenti, the scheme’s finance partner.

How to Apply

  1. Confirm eligibility: ≥18 years, permanent resident, Synergy/Horizon customer, WA property with internet, willing to join a VPP.
  2. Read Consumer Protection guidance for solar/battery purchases to ensure fair contracts and warranties.
  3. Obtain quotes from approved vendors (they handle all rebate and VPP enrolment).
  4. Installer lodges state & federal claims – rebates are applied to your invoice post‑installation.
  5. Complete loan application (optional): Plenti contacts you for finance approval and disbursal.

For full details, the vendor list, and FAQs, visit wa.gov.au/BatteryScheme or email batteryrebate@deed.wa.gov.au.

For more information on the scheme, visit WA Residential Battery Scheme – Information for Applicants.
